Mahatma Gandhi

Born: October 2, 1869 | Died: January 30, 1948

Father of the Nation. Led India's independence movement against British rule through non-violent resistance (Satyagraha).

Jawaharlal Nehru

Born: November 14, 1889 | Died: May 27, 1964

First Prime Minister of India. Vision for a modern, secular, and democratic India. Played a crucial role in India's independence struggle.

Indira Gandhi

Born: November 19, 1917 | Died: October 31, 1984

First female Prime Minister of India. Served for 15 years and was instrumental in shaping modern India's foreign policy.

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

Born: October 31, 1875 | Died: December 15, 1950

Iron Man of India. Unified over 560 princely states to form the Indian Union after independence.

Dr. Ambedkar

Born: April 14, 1891 | Died: December 6, 1956

Chief Architect of the Indian Constitution. Social reformer and advocate for the rights of Dalit communities in India.

Sachin Tendulkar

Born: December 24, 1973 | Active: 1989-2013

Cricket legend. Considered the greatest batsman of all time. Played 200 Test matches and 463 ODIs for India.

About India

India is a diverse and vibrant country located in South Asia. It is the world's largest democracy with over 1.4 billion people.

The country is known for its rich history, diverse culture, and contributions to art, science, and philosophy.

India is home to various religions including Hinduism, Buddhism, Islam, and Sikhism.

The Indian economy is one of the largest in the world, with a strong IT sector and growing industries.

Indian cuisine is world-famous for its spices, flavors, and regional diversity.

Bollywood, the Indian film industry, is known globally and produces thousands of movies annually.

India's contributions to mathematics include the concept of zero and the decimal system.

The Taj Mahal, built by Emperor Shah Jahan, is one of the Seven Wonders of the World.

India has a rich tradition of classical arts including dance forms like Bharatanatyam and Kathak.

The Indian flag has three colors: saffron, white, and green, with a blue wheel in the center.

India's national animal is the Bengal Tiger, a symbol of wildlife conservation.

The Indian climate varies from tropical in the south to temperate in the north.

India is known for its festival celebrations including Diwali, Holi, and Durga Puja.

The country has 28 states and 8 union territories with their own unique cultures.

Sanskrit, one of India's ancient languages, is considered the mother of many Indo-European languages.

Indian traditional medicine, Ayurveda, is practiced worldwide for holistic health.

India's role in global affairs has grown significantly in the 21st century.

The country is a major exporter of software services and IT talent globally.

India celebrates Independence Day on August 15th, marking freedom from British rule.

The Indian subcontinent is home to diverse ecosystems from the Himalayas to tropical forests.
